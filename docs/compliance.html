1. Compliance stance

TrustChain provides technical evidence primitives: signed tool outputs, replay-aware envelopes, `.tcreceipt` portable proofs, chain verification, standards exports, and external anchors. It does not replace a compliance program, risk management process, or legal review.

Honest claim: TrustChain makes AI execution evidence verifiable. It does not automatically make a system SOC 2, HIPAA, DORA, FDA, or EU AI Act compliant.

3. Evidence kit

Receipt

.tcreceipt proves a specific tool output was signed by a key and can be checked offline.

Chain

tc chain-verify detects broken parent links and historical tampering inside the local chain.

Anchor

tc anchor export stores a chain-head checkpoint outside the agent's writable environment.

Standards

tc standards export emits SCITT-shaped JSON, W3C VC envelopes, or in-toto Statements.

Identity

Tool and agent certificates bind signatures to organizational identity and revocation state.

Report

Pro/Enterprise can package receipts, anchors, policy decisions, and CRL snapshots for auditors.

6. Commands

tc anchor verify chain.anchor.json -d .trustchain
tc standards export result.tcreceipt --format scitt -o result.air.json
tc standards export result.tcreceipt --format w3c-vc -o result.vc.json
tc standards export result.tcreceipt --format intoto -o result.intoto.json
tc-verify trustchain_chain.jsonl.gz --pubkey BASE64_PUBLIC_KEY

7. Limits

  • A valid signature proves origin and integrity of bytes, not real-world truth.
  • A local chain is tamper-evident, not globally tamper-proof, unless anchored externally.
  • Receipts without pinned keys prove internal consistency, not organizational identity.
  • Regulatory compliance depends on deployment, controls, documentation, and legal interpretation.
